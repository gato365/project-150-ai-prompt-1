One-Way ANOVA: Within & Between Group Variability

Your goal in this activity is to develop an understanding of two key ideas in one-way ANOVA. Work through the questions below using your assigned method.

The core question of ANOVA: When we observe differences between group means, are those differences real — or just noise? The answer depends on comparing two types of variability.
Question 1 — Within-Group Variability
Consider three groups of students who studied using different methods. Within each group, scores vary. In your own words: what does within-group variability capture? Why does it matter?
Question 2 — Between-Group Variability
Now consider the three group means. One group averaged 72, another 85, another 91. What does between-group variability capture? How is it different from within-group variability?
Question 3 — The F-Ratio
The test statistic for ANOVA is the F-ratio: F = Between / Within. Based on what you've learned, describe what it would mean if F >> 1. What about F ≈ 1? Can F be less than 0?
Question 4 — Equal Variance Condition
For ANOVA to work correctly, we assume within-group variability is roughly equal across groups. Explain in your own words: why does this condition matter? What goes wrong if it's violated?
